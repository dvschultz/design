Over the past three years the NY Times Storytelling team has rebuilt its article product from scratch to put the news front and center. The goals for the project were to create a system that supported more forms of storytelling, to support every digital device (responsive, mobile apps, and 3rd party systems), and to increase revenue (from both advertising and subscriptions). Discovery Research

Research Lead: Anjeli Sharma
Designers: Peter Rentz, Jasmine Wiggins
Product: Lauren Chinn, Priya Ollapally Wellington

Over three months we interviewed readers across the US and abroad to better understand their news reading habits and expectations. We spoke with newsroom stakeholders about their current frustrations with the article experience and hope for future opportunities. My role was to synthesize the information gathered, lead brainstorms, and design artifacts for our provocation process. This would guide the rest of our story work for the next two years.

Opinion Design Sprints

Designers: Peter Rentz, Javeria Ali

Product: Tyson Evans, Frances Hannan

In an increasingly polarizing political climate the Times realized a need to better differentiate our opinion coverage from our news content. How do we let readers know that these pieces come from completely separate editing systems with their own rules and norms? How do we help readers recognize the differences at first glance?

Breaking News Sprint

Designers: Javeria Ali

Product: Frances Hannan

Research: Maura Youngman, Ksenia Pachikov

We knew breaking news readers predominantly broke into two groups:

  1. Readers who wanted to check in immediately about a breaking news topic
  2. Readers who might return days after the event to understand what occurred
