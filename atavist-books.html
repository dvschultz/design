    • Atavist Books

    Atavist Books was founded by IAC’s Barry Diller, producer Scott Rudin, publisher Frances Coady, and digital magazine The Atavist. It only lasted a few short years, but was possibly the most ambituous digital book publisher during its lifespan.

    My role as Digital Design and Production Manager was to oversee digital production for our titles. I built custom software and workflows to convert each title from Word doc or InDesign files into JSON to be imported into Creativist (later renamed Atavist). Every book was published on the Creatavist platform for the optimum experience, but was also available as EPUB and Kindle titles for gracefully degraded reading.

    While Chip Kidd was the creative director for most Atavist Books projects, I led the interactive experiences on many of the titles and additional marketing projects.

    Sleep Donation Trailer

    Sleep Donation was the inaugural Atavist Books title. Written by MacArthur Grant winner Karen Russell, the novella wasn’t an interactive title but promotional materials for the story explored TKTKTK. Sleep Donation’s cover was created by Chip Kidd and illustrated by TK. We had TK produce additional illustrations for the trailer which I animated.
